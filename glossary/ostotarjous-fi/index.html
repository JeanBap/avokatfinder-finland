Ostotarjous (Purchase Offer)

An ostotarjous is a written purchase offer for Finnish property. When accepted by the seller, it creates a binding preliminary commitment — breaking it triggers penalty clauses. Understanding what you are signing before you submit is critical.

In the Finnish property market, making an ostotarjous (purchase offer) is a significant legal act. Unlike informal expressions of interest in some markets, a Finnish ostotarjous is a binding document — once signed by the buyer and accepted by the seller, both parties are committed. The typical penalty for withdrawing without valid grounds is 2–4% of the purchase price.

For As Oy apartment purchases, the ostotarjous is followed by the kauppakirja (purchase agreement). For real property (kiinteisto), the ostotarjous is a preliminary commitment and the formal kauppakirja is signed before a public purchase witness to complete the transfer.

Finnish offers typically include conditions (ehdot) that allow the buyer to withdraw without penalty if specific conditions are not met: financing approval (rahoitusehto), satisfactory kuntotarkastus (condition survey), or satisfactory review of the isannoitsijantodistus. These conditions must be explicitly stated in the offer — verbal conditions are not recognised.

The rahoitusehto (financing condition) is the most common. It states that the purchase is conditional on the buyer obtaining mortgage approval from their bank within a specified number of days. If the bank declines, the buyer can withdraw without penalty. The condition must be exercised in good faith — using a pre-approved finance status and then inventing a bank refusal to get out of the deal is not permissible.

Estate agents in Finland are licensed professionals regulated by the Regional State Administrative Agency (Aluehallintovirasto). They prepare the ostotarjous documentation and are required to explain its binding nature to both parties before signing.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Submitting an ostotarjous without a financing condition when your mortgage is not yet approved is a high-risk strategy. If the bank declines, you owe the penalty. Always include the rahoitusehto unless you are a cash buyer.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Negotiate the conditions period generously. A standard kuntotarkastus takes 3–5 working days to arrange. If you need 10 days for the bank to approve finance, write 10 days into the offer — do not accept a shorter window under sales pressure.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I submit an ostotarjous below the asking price?

Yes. Finnish property is not subject to a compulsory auction system like Norway. Private negotiation before and alongside a formal offer is common, particularly in slower markets.

What happens if the seller rejects my offer?

The offer lapses. The seller can counter-offer. Until the seller signs acceptance, neither party is bound — the commitment only crystallises on mutual signature.

Can the seller withdraw after accepting my offer?

Technically the agreement is binding on acceptance. A withdrawing seller faces the same penalty as a withdrawing buyer — typically returning double the deposit (kahden kerrantalletuksen palautus) or equivalent financial penalty.
