Varainsiirtovero (Transfer Tax)

Varainsiirtovero is Finland's property transfer tax: 4% on real property (land and buildings) and 2% on housing company shares (As Oy). First-time buyers of As Oy shares are exempt under certain conditions.

Finland levies transfer tax (varainsiirtovero) on all property purchases. The rate differs depending on what is being purchased: 4% applies to real property (kiinteisto) — land and any directly owned building; 2% applies to housing company shares (asunto-osakeyhtio shares). This distinction is important because most Finnish apartments are shares, not real property.

First-time buyers of As Oy shares who are aged between 18 and 39 and commit to using the property as their primary residence are fully exempt from the 2% transfer tax. This can represent a saving of several thousand euros. The exemption must be claimed at the time of purchase — it cannot be reclaimed retroactively.

For real property purchases, no first-time buyer exemption exists — all purchasers pay 4%. The buyer is responsible for filing the transfer tax return (varainsiirtoveroilmoitus) and paying the tax within two months of the purchase date.

In practice, banks typically require the transfer tax to be paid before releasing mortgage funds for As Oy purchases. The payment confirmation is also needed for the share transfer registration in the osakehuoneistorekisteri.

New build purchases directly from a developer are often subject to VAT (arvonlisavero) instead of transfer tax on the construction portion. The land component may still attract transfer tax. Always clarify with the developer and your lawyer which taxes apply to a specific new-build purchase.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Claiming the first-time buyer exemption requires filing the correct declaration at the time of purchase. If you forget to claim it and pay the 2%, you can apply for a refund — but within a strict two-year time limit.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Confirm with your bank whether they will advance mortgage funds before or after transfer tax is paid. Some banks require proof of payment first, meaning you need the tax money available separately from the mortgage.

Frequently Asked Questions

What counts as real property vs As Oy shares?

A detached house on its own plot is typically kiinteisto (real property) at 4%. A flat in a block is almost always As Oy shares at 2%. Semi-detached houses can be either — check the sales brochure and legal documents.

At what age does the first-time buyer exemption expire?

The buyer must be under 40 at the time of purchase. If you turn 40 before completing, you lose the exemption.

Can I claim the exemption if I already own a summer cottage?

The exemption requires that you have not previously owned at least 50% of a residential property (asunto) used as a primary dwelling. Summer cottages (loma-asunto) do not count — they are not primary residences.
