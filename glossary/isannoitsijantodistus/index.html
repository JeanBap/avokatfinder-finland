Isännöitsijäntodistus

Isännöitsijäntodistus Finland – Property Manager's Certificate for Flat Purchases

The isännöitsijäntodistus (property manager's certificate) is a standardised document issued by the isännöitsijä (property manager) of a Finnish asunto-osakeyhtiö (housing company) containing all material information about the housing company and the specific flat (asunto-osake). It is mandatory for all asunto-osake sales under the Asuntokauppalaki.

The isännöitsijäntodistus contains: the housing company's name and registration number, the flat's share numbers and floor area, any unpaid charges owed by the flat's owner (hoitovastike, rahoitusvastike), planned major renovations (peruskorjaukset), the housing company's total debt and loan schedule, and the current maintenance fee levels.

One of the most critical items in the isännöitsijäntodistus is the upcoming renovation plan (tuleva remontti or taloyhtiön korjaussuunnitelma). Major renovations — particularly putkiremontti (pipe renovation) and julkisivuremontti (facade renovation) — are expensive and can cost the flat owner tens of thousands of euros in rahoitusvastike (financing charges). An upcoming pipe renovation in a 1960s or 1970s building is a major price factor.

The isännöitsijäntodistus also discloses any outstanding rahoitusvastike (housing company loan repayment charge) relating to the specific flat. This charge, like Norwegian fellesgjeld, represents the flat's share of the housing company's loans. The totalprice must include both the purchase price and the outstanding rahoitusvastike.

The certificate must be current (typically not older than 3 months) at the time of sale and is usually arranged by the estate agent. Buyers should review it carefully rather than accepting the agent's summary — the agent has an interest in the sale proceeding.

⚠ Common MistakeNot asking whether a putkiremontti is planned within the next ten years. A pipe renovation can cost EUR 20,000–50,000 per flat — a fact that should heavily influence offer price in older buildings.
💡 Expert TipCompare the rahoitusvastike per m² across properties you are considering. A flat with a low purchase price but high rahoitusvastike and upcoming putkiremontti may be more expensive in total than a newer flat with a higher headline price.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between hoitovastike and rahoitusvastike?

Hoitovastike is the monthly maintenance charge covering operating costs. Rahoitusvastike is the loan repayment charge — what you pay toward the housing company's debt. Both appear in the isännöitsijäntodistus.

Can I pay off the rahoitusvastike in a lump sum?

Yes, in most housing companies. Paying off the share of the housing company's loan (lunastaminen) eliminates the ongoing rahoitusvastike. The lump-sum amount is shown in the isännöitsijäntodistus.

Is the isännöitsijäntodistus available to prospective buyers before the sale?

Yes. It is standard practice to make it available during the property viewing period (näyttö). If an agent will not provide it before an offer, that is a red flag.
