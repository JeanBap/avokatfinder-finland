Kiinteistövero

Kiinteistövero Finland – Annual Real Estate Tax for Property Owners

Kiinteistövero (real estate tax) is Finland's annual tax on the ownership of real property (kiinteistöt — land and buildings on privately owned land, as distinct from asunto-osakkeet in housing companies). It is set by each municipality within national minimum and maximum rates and is payable by the property owner as of 1 January of the tax year.

The kiinteistövero is based on the official assessed value (verotusarvo) of the property set by the Finnish Tax Administration (Verohallinto). The assessment values were significantly reformed in a major revaluation starting from 2025, which will be phased in over several years. The new system aims to bring assessments closer to market value, resulting in higher tax bills for many property owners.

Municipal kiinteistövero rates vary by property type and locality. For general real estate (non-residential land and buildings), rates range from 0.93% to 2.00%. For permanent residences, reduced rates of 0.41% to 1.00% apply. Holiday homes and investment properties face the higher general rates. Municipalities set their rates annually within these bands.

Kiinteistövero applies to kiinteistöt (real property with land title) — it does not apply directly to asunto-osakkeet (housing company shares). Asunto-osakeyhtiöt (the housing companies themselves) pay kiinteistövero on the buildings they own, and this cost is reflected in the hoitovastike charged to members.

Kiinteistövero is assessed and billed directly by Verohallinto based on property register data. Property owners do not need to file a return — they receive a tax bill based on registered ownership. Bills are due in two instalments (August and October) for amounts over a minimum threshold.

⚠ Common MistakeNot factoring the 2025 revaluation into investment property acquisition models. For properties where assessed value has historically been well below market value, the reform may materially increase annual kiinteistövero.
💡 Expert TipObtain the current verotusarvo and current municipality rate for any investment property you are analysing. Verohallinto publishes municipal rates, and the verotusarvo is available in the property assessment documentation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is kiinteistövero deductible against rental income?

Yes. For rental properties, kiinteistövero is deductible against Finnish rental income.

Can I appeal my kiinteistövero assessment?

Yes. Property owners can request a correction from Verohallinto if they believe the assessed value is incorrect. The deadline for appeal is typically within five years of the assessment year.

Does kiinteistövero apply to forest land?

Yes, but at significantly reduced rates. Forest land has its own assessment methodology and favourable kiinteistövero rates as part of Finnish forest ownership policy.
