Kauppakirja

Kauppakirja is the Finnish term for the deed of sale — the legally binding document that transfers ownership of real property or housing company shares from seller to buyer.

In Finnish property transactions, the kauppakirja is the central legal instrument. For real property (kiinteistö), the deed must be executed before a public purchase witness (julkinen kaupanvahvistaja) for the transfer to be legally valid and registrable.

A valid kauppakirja for real estate must contain the full names and identity numbers of both parties, a precise description of the property (including cadastral ID), the agreed purchase price, any conditions or easements, and signatures witnessed by the public purchase witness.

For apartments in housing companies (asunto-osakeyhtiö), the kauppakirja does not require a public witness, but the transaction must be reported to the housing company and the shares formally transferred in the share register. The buyer typically pays stamp duty within 2 months.

Finnish law requires that any kauppakirja for real estate be presented to the National Land Survey of Finland (Maanmittauslaitos) for registration of ownership (lainhuuto). Failure to register within six months results in penalty fees.

Common Mistake: Confusing the kauppakirja for a housing company share with a real estate deed — the former requires no public witness, but the latter is invalid without one, meaning the transfer cannot be registered.
Expert Tip: Ensure the kauppakirja includes a clear clause on who bears any outstanding housing company loans (yhtiölaina), as these affect the true cost of acquisition and stamp duty calculations.

Buyers unfamiliar with Finnish property law should engage a local lawyer to review the kauppakirja before signing, particularly for real estate transactions requiring the public witness.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a julkinen kaupanvahvistaja in Finland?

A julkinen kaupanvahvistaja is a public purchase witness authorised to confirm real estate transfers. Without their signature, a Finnish real estate kauppakirja is legally void.

How long do I have to register a Finnish property purchase?

You must apply for lainhuuto (ownership registration) at Maanmittauslaitos within six months of signing the kauppakirja. Late registration incurs a penalty fee equal to 20% of the transfer tax.

Is stamp duty paid on Finnish housing company share purchases?

Yes. The transfer tax (varainsiirtovero) is 2% of the purchase price for asunto-osake shares, compared to 4% for direct real estate purchases.
