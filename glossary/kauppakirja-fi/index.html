What is Kauppakirja (Finnish Purchase Deed)?

The binding purchase deed for Finnish real estate — must be signed before a public purchase witness (julkinen kaupanvahvistaja) to be legally valid. Triggers varainsiirtovero payment and lainhuuto registration.

Key Facts — Kauppakirja (Finnish Purchase Deed)
  • Formal requirement: written + signed by both parties + JKV witness
  • JKV: licensed estate agent, notary, or bank official
  • Triggers: 2-month window for varainsiirtovero; 6-month window for lainhuuto
  • AsOy shares: JKV not required, lighter formalities
  • Remote witnessing: available under Finnish law reforms

The kauppakirja is the binding purchase deed for Finnish real estate. Under Finnish property law (Maakaari — Land Code), a valid kauppakirja must be in writing, signed by both seller and buyer, and countersigned by a julkinen kaupanvahvistaja (public purchase witness). The witness confirms the parties' identities and the authenticity of the signatures. Without the witness signature, the kauppakirja is legally void.

The julkinen kaupanvahvistaja (JKV) is an authorised public official who can witness Finnish real estate purchase deeds. JKV authorisation is granted to certain professionals: licensed estate agents (LKV-qualified kiinteistovalittaja), bank officials, notaries, and some lawyers. Finnish law reforms have expanded remote witnessing significantly, making it easier for non-residents to execute deeds without travelling to Finland.

The kauppakirja must include: the full legal description of the property (kiinteistotunnus); the parties' names and henkilotunnus or equivalent identifier; the purchase price and payment terms; any conditions precedent; the completion date (hallinnansiirtopaiva); and any warranties or disclosures.

For Finnish asunto-osakeyhtio share transfers, the formal requirements are lighter — a written share transfer agreement must be signed but does not require a JKV witness. However, the AsOy board must be notified, the Osakehuoneistorekisteri must be updated, and the 2% varainsiirtovero must be paid within two months.

After the kauppakirja is signed, the buyer must: pay the varainsiirtovero within two months; submit the lainhuuto application within six months; and arrange for the release of any prior kiinnitykset that the seller agreed to discharge.

Common Mistake: Foreign buyers sometimes execute a Finnish real estate purchase deed without a julkinen kaupanvahvistaja. An unwitnessed deed is legally void — the transfer has no legal effect and lainhuuto cannot be applied for.
Expert Tip: Finland allows remote JKV witnessing through authorised services following 2023 legislative reforms. Several Finnish property law firms and estate agencies now offer remote kauppakirja witnessing for non-resident buyers.
Related terms: LAINHUUTO VARAINSIIRTOVERO ASUNTO-OSAKEYHTIO

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I sign a Finnish kauppakirja remotely?

Yes — Finnish law reforms in 2023 expanded remote witnessing options. Depending on the service provider, you may sign with Finnish digital authentication tools, via a notarised video witnessing procedure, or through a Finnish lawyer with power of attorney.

Is a preliminary agreement (esisopimus) binding in Finland?

A Finnish esisopimus can be made binding if it meets the same formal requirements as a kauppakirja — written, signed by both parties, JKV witnessed. If it lacks any formal requirement, it is not a binding real estate agreement.

What happens if the seller fails to complete a Finnish property purchase?

If a seller refuses to complete after a valid kauppakirja is signed, the buyer can apply for a specific performance order compelling the transfer. Finnish law allows courts to compel property transfers.
