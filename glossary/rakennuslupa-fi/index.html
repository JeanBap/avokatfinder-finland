Rakennuslupa

Rakennuslupa Finland – Building Permit Requirements and Process

A rakennuslupa (building permit) is required in Finland before commencing construction of new buildings, major extensions, or significant structural alterations under the Land Use and Building Act (Maankäyttö- ja rakennuslaki, MRL). It is issued by the local municipality's building supervisory authority (rakennusvalvontaviranomainen).

The permit application must be signed by a pääsuunnittelija (lead designer) — a qualified architect or engineer who takes professional responsibility for the design's compliance with regulations. The pääsuunnittelija is a distinct role from the structural engineer (rakennesuunnittelija) and other specialist designers. Their involvement is mandatory.

Applications are submitted with architectural drawings, a site plan (asemapiirros), structural calculations, energy performance documentation, and proof of land ownership or right to use. The municipality checks compliance with the local detailed plan (asemakaava) and national building code (Suomen rakentamismääräyskokoelma).

Key Facts

Processing time4–8 weeks typical
CompletionLoppukatselmus (final inspection) required

Processing times vary by municipality — typically four to eight weeks for standard residential permits. Helsinki's Rakennusvalvonta handles applications digitally via the Lupapiste platform, which has reduced processing times for straightforward applications. Rural municipalities may be slower.

After construction is complete, the builder must request a final inspection (loppukatselmus) from the rakennusvalvonta. Only after passing the loppukatselmus is the building legally ready for occupation. The building is then registered in the population register's building data (rakennustiedot in Väestörekisterikeskus).

⚠ Common MistakeCommencing construction without a rakennuslupa, relying on a neighbour's assurance that no permit is needed. Only the municipality can confirm what permits are required — and construction without a required permit risks demolition orders (purkamismääräys).
💡 Expert TipUse the Lupapiste.fi platform to check what permit types apply to your project across Finnish municipalities. Many minor works require only a toimenpidelupa (action permit) or the municipality's notification acknowledgement — not a full rakennuslupa — reducing cost and processing time.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between rakennuslupa and toimenpidelupa?

A toimenpidelupa (action permit) is a simplified permit for works that are significant but do not require a full building permit — such as substantial exterior alterations, large antennas, or significant interior works. The MRL specifies which works fall into which category.

Is a rakennuslupa required for solar panels in Finland?

Typically a toimenpidelupa or local consultation is needed for most solar installations, not a full rakennuslupa. However, heritage-listed buildings and conservation areas have stricter requirements.

How long is a Finnish rakennuslupa valid?

Three years from grant to commence construction, and five years in total to complete the work. Extensions can be granted in justified circumstances.
