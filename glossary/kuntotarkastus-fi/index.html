Kuntotarkastus (Building Condition Survey)

A kuntotarkastus is a professional building condition survey in Finland, strongly recommended before purchasing a detached house (omakotitalo). It is distinct from the isannoitsijantodistus used for As Oy apartments.

For detached houses and terraced houses classified as real property (kiinteisto), the equivalent of due diligence is the kuntotarkastus (condition survey). Unlike As Oy shares — where the company's documentation and isannoitsijantodistus take priority — real property purchases require a physical building inspection to assess the structure, roof, foundation, moisture, and systems.

The kuntotarkastus is conducted by a licensed building surveyor (rakennustarkastaja or kuntoarviokija) and rated according to a standardised Finnish system. Results are graded using risk classifications: no defect, minor observation, recommendation for further investigation (jatkotutkimussuositus), or serious defect requiring immediate action.

A jatkotutkimussuositus is a common finding that indicates a potential concern — such as moisture risk in a crawl space or around a chimney base — that cannot be confirmed without invasive investigation. The cost and scope of the further investigation falls to the buyer unless the purchase is made conditional on a satisfactory result.

Since Finnish property is sold 'as is' (myydaan sellaisena kuin se on), the seller cannot be held liable for defects that were visible during the kuntotarkastus or reasonably discoverable. However, sellers remain liable for concealed defects they knew about and failed to disclose. A properly documented kuntotarkastus protects both parties by creating an agreed baseline of the property's condition at the time of sale.

The cost of a kuntotarkastus typically ranges from EUR 400–800 depending on the property size and complexity. This is a negligible sum relative to the liability it helps identify or allocate.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Skipping the kuntotarkastus on a detached house purchase to save EUR 500–800 is a false economy. A single moisture issue in a Finnish basement can cost EUR 20,000–100,000 to remediate.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: If the kuntotarkastus returns multiple jatkotutkimussuositus flags, negotiate the purchase price downwards or make the sale conditional on the seller funding the further investigations. Accepting all risk without investigation is a common buyer regret.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between kuntotarkastus and kuntoraportti?

A kuntoraportti is the written report produced from a kuntotarkastus inspection. The terms are often used interchangeably, but strictly speaking the survey is the inspection and the raportti is the document.

Does the seller have to provide a kuntotarkastus?

No. There is no legal obligation on the seller to commission one. But without it, the seller bears greater liability for hidden defects — so most serious sellers commission one proactively.

Can I commission my own kuntotarkastus?

Yes. Buyers can commission their own survey independently of any seller-provided report. An independent survey gives you an unbiased view not influenced by the seller's interests.
