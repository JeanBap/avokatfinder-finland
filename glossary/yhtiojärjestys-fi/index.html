Yhtiöjärjestys

Yhtiöjärjestys Finland – Articles of Association in Housing Companies

The yhtiöjärjestys (articles of association) is the constitutional document of a Finnish asunto-osakeyhtiö (housing company). It defines the fundamental rules of the company — including the allocation of flats to specific share series, maintenance responsibilities, use restrictions, and any consent requirements for alterations. Every Finnish housing company must have a yhtiöjärjestys.

The yhtiöjärjestys specifies which shares correspond to which flat (osakeryhmä), the flat's floor area, and the allocation of maintenance responsibility between the housing company and individual shareholders. These maintenance responsibility rules determine whether a shareholder or the housing company bears the cost of plumbing, electrical installations, or surface materials within the flat.

Use restrictions in the yhtiöjärjestys are binding on all shareholders. Common restrictions include: prohibition on operating business premises in residential flats, limitations on the number of occupants, pet policies, and rules on short-term rentals (such as Airbnb). Violations can lead to a warning notice (varoitus) and ultimately a claim for eviction (lunastuskanne).

The yhtiöjärjestys may also contain consent requirements for alterations. Major renovations within a flat — particularly those affecting load-bearing structures, plumbing, or electrical systems — typically require the housing company's prior approval. Unapproved alterations can be ordered reversed at the shareholder's cost.

Buyers should request the current yhtiöjärjestys as part of the pre-sale documentation and review it carefully alongside the isännöitsijäntodistus. Together, these two documents provide a complete picture of the rights and obligations associated with the flat.

⚠ Common MistakeAssuming alteration works within the flat are freely permitted without reading the yhtiöjärjestys. A failure to get housing company approval for alterations affecting plumbing or structure can result in costly restoration orders.
💡 Expert TipAsk the estate agent specifically whether any alterations to the flat were done during the current ownership and whether housing company approval was obtained. Unapproved alterations are the seller's problem but become yours on purchase.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a yhtiöjärjestys be changed?

Yes, but changes require a two-thirds majority vote at a general meeting. Some changes affecting individual shareholders' rights require unanimous consent.

What is lunastuskanne?

A claim by the housing company to compulsorily purchase a shareholder's shares — an extreme remedy available in cases of repeated serious violations of the yhtiöjärjestys or the Asunto-osakeyhtiölaki.

Does the yhtiöjärjestys override the Asunto-osakeyhtiölaki?

The yhtiöjärjestys cannot override mandatory provisions of the Asunto-osakeyhtiölaki. It can provide stricter rules than the statutory minimum, but not weaker ones where the Act mandates minimum protections.
