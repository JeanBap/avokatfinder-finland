Isannoitsijantodistus (Property Manager Certificate)

The isannoitsijantodistus is the definitive due-diligence document for Finnish As Oy apartment purchases. It discloses the housing company's finances, debt, upcoming renovations, and the apartment's share details — and must be provided before any binding sale.

The isannoitsijantodistus (property manager's certificate) is issued by the isannoitsija (property manager) of the asunto-osakeyhtio and is the foundation of all As Oy apartment due diligence. The Housing Companies Act requires sellers to provide it to buyers before signing a binding purchase agreement.

The certificate discloses: the apartment's floor area and share numbers; the current hoitovastike (maintenance charge); the current rahoitusvastike (financial charge for company loans); any outstanding company loans (taloyhtion laina) and the apartment's proportional share; unpaid charges by the current owner; and planned major renovations with estimated costs and timelines.

Major renovations of particular concern include: putkiremontti (pipe overhaul), julkisivuremontti (facade renovation), kattoremontit (roof works), and LVIA-remontti (plumbing and HVAC). Each can cost tens of thousands of euros per apartment. The isannoitsijantodistus will note whether these are planned, budgeted, or under discussion at the board level.

The isannoitsijantodistus also states whether the company has an energy certificate (energiatodistus), which is required for sales of apartments in buildings constructed before 1980 and for all sales of individually metered units. An outdated or missing energiatodistus can block a sale.

Buyers should treat planned renovations as a deduction from the property's value and factor the associated rahoitusvastike increases into their monthly cost calculations. A flat listed at EUR 200,000 with an incoming EUR 30,000 putkiremontti share has a true acquisition cost of EUR 230,000.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Reading only the monthly hoitovastike without checking the rahoitusvastike gives a false picture of monthly costs. Both charges together form the true monthly obligation, and the rahoitusvastike can exceed the hoitovastike when a major renovation loan is active.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Ask for the housing company's most recent tilinpaatos (annual accounts) and talousarvio (budget) in addition to the isannoitsijantodistus. The accounts reveal whether the company is building adequate reserves or deferring maintenance.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get the isannoitsijantodistus?

The seller is responsible for providing it. You can also request it directly from the isannoitsija (their details are on the certificate) for a small fee if you want an independent copy.

What is the difference between hoitovastike and rahoitusvastike?

Hoitovastike (maintenance charge) covers day-to-day running costs. Rahoitusvastike (financial charge) repays company renovation loans. Hoitovastike is ongoing; rahoitusvastike exists only when the company has active loans and ceases once they are repaid.

Can the renovation costs stated in the certificate change?

Yes. The figures are estimates and actual costs often exceed initial budgets. For a renovation in planning stage, treat the figure as a minimum rather than a ceiling.
