Panttikirja

A panttikirja is a Finnish mortgage deed — a document issued by the National Land Survey of Finland representing a right to pledge real property as security for a loan.

When borrowing against Finnish real estate, the lender requires a panttikirja as collateral. The panttikirja is not the mortgage itself but a document evidencing a right to mortgage up to a stated amount against a specific property. It is held by the lender until the loan is repaid.

Since 2014, Finland has operated an electronic panttikirja system (sähköinen panttikirja). Physical paper deeds issued before 2014 remain valid but can be converted to digital form. Electronic deeds are held in the Maanmittauslaitos register and cannot be lost or destroyed.

A single property may have multiple panttikirjat of different denominations. Lenders are ranked by priority — earlier-registered panttikirjat take precedence in enforcement. Buyers should check existing panttikirjat to understand the property's encumbrance position.

When a Finnish mortgage is fully repaid, the lender returns the panttikirja to the owner. The owner can then pledge it again for a new loan without applying for a new deed, saving costs. This reusability makes the panttikirja system efficient.

Common Mistake: Assuming that repaying a Finnish mortgage automatically cancels the panttikirja. The deed remains valid and in the owner's possession after repayment — it can be reused for future borrowing without additional cost.
Expert Tip: Before making an offer on Finnish real estate, request a register extract (lainhuuto- ja rasitustodistus) showing all existing panttikirjat and their priority order. This reveals the full secured debt picture.

Finnish homeowners who have repaid their mortgage should store their panttikirja securely — or leave it with their bank for safekeeping — as it retains value as future borrowing collateral.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use an old paper panttikirja as mortgage security in Finland?

Yes. Paper panttikirjat issued before 2014 remain legally valid as mortgage security. However, lenders may prefer conversion to electronic form for administrative ease.

What happens to the panttikirja when I sell my Finnish property?

The panttikirja stays with the property, not the person. At sale, outstanding panttikirjat held by lenders must be released (the underlying loan repaid) or transferred to the new owner's financing arrangement.

How do I apply for a new panttikirja in Finland?

Applications are submitted to Maanmittauslaitos, either online through the eServices portal or in writing. The fee for issuing a new electronic panttikirja is approximately €119.
