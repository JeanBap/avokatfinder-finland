Myyntivoittovero

Myyntivoittovero Finland – Capital Gains Tax on Property Sales

Myyntivoittovero (capital gains tax — literally "profit from sale tax") is Finland's tax on gains realised from selling real property or housing company shares. The rate is 30% on gains up to EUR 30,000 and 34% on the portion above EUR 30,000. It is reported on the annual Verohallinto tax return for the year of sale.

The most important exemption is the oman asunnon luovutusvoitto (own home capital gain exemption). Gains from selling a property that has been used as the seller's primary residence are fully exempt if: (a) the seller owned the property for at least two years, and (b) the property served as the seller's primary residence for at least two years during the ownership period (they need not be the last two years).

For properties that do not qualify for the own-home exemption, the taxable gain is: sale price minus acquisition cost minus documented improvement costs (not maintenance) minus transaction costs (agent fees, legal fees). No indexation of historic costs is applied in Finland (unlike Denmark), so long-held properties face significant nominal gains in rising markets.

Key Facts

Tax rate30% (≤EUR 30k gain) / 34% (>EUR 30k gain)
Own home exemption2 years ownership + 2 years primary residence
DeductibleAcquisition + improvement + transaction costs
Loss carry-forward5 years against capital income
Inherited propertyGain from probate value, not original cost

Gains on inherited or gifted property are calculated from the probate value (perintöverotusarvo) or gift tax value (lahjaverotusarvo), not the original purchase price. This can result in substantially lower gains — or losses — depending on when the property was inherited relative to when the heir sells it.

Capital losses on property sales can be deducted against capital gains in the same year. Any remaining net loss is deductible against other capital income (pääomatulot). Capital losses not offset in the year of sale can be carried forward for five years against capital income.

⚠ Common MistakeAssuming the two-year residence period must be the last two years of ownership. The two years of primary residence can be any two years during the ownership period — enabling sellers who have moved out but owned for years to still qualify.
💡 Expert TipDocument all improvement costs from day one. Finnish tax law is strict about the distinction between maintenance (ei vähennettävissä — not deductible) and improvement (vähennettävissä — deductible). A new kitchen replacing an old one is partly improvement; repainting is maintenance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the own-home exemption apply to a property held through a housing company?

Yes. Asunto-osakkeet (housing company shares) for owner-occupied flats qualify for the same own-home exemption as real property, subject to the same conditions.

What if I sell a holiday home?

Holiday homes do not qualify for the own-home exemption. Gains on holiday home sales are fully taxable at 30%/34%.

Is myyntivoittovero withheld at source?

No. The seller reports the gain on the Verohallinto annual return and pays the tax as part of the normal tax settlement. Sellers receiving large proceeds should ensure they have sufficient prepayments to avoid a large end-of-year bill.
