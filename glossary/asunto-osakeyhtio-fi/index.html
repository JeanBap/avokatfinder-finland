Asunto-osakeyhtio (Finnish Housing Company)

An asunto-osakeyhtio (Oy) is the most common form of apartment ownership in Finland. Buyers purchase shares in the housing company rather than the apartment itself, making the company's financial health as important as the apartment's condition.

Finland has one of the world's most unique housing ownership structures: the vast majority of Finnish apartments are owned through an asunto-osakeyhtio (housing company, often abbreviated As Oy). Rather than owning the bricks and mortar of your apartment, you own a specified number of shares in the company that owns the building. Those shares give you the exclusive right to occupy a particular apartment.

The asunto-osakeyhtio is governed by the Asunto-osakeyhtiölaki (Housing Companies Act) and managed by an elected board (hallitus) with day-to-day operations handled by an isannoitsija (property manager). Major decisions — including taking out loans for renovations — require approval from the general meeting (yhtiokokous) of shareholders.

Every potential buyer should request the isannoitsijantodistus (property manager's certificate) before making an offer. This document discloses: the share range for the apartment, outstanding company debt (taloyhtion velka) per share, upcoming large repairs (putkiremontti, julkisivuremontti, etc.), the current maintenance charge (hoitovastike), and any unpaid charges by the current owner.

The isannoitsijantodistus is the single most important due-diligence document in a Finnish property purchase. Unlike in many countries where the physical survey is paramount, in Finland the company's financial and renovation plan documentation is equally — often more — critical.

Shares in an asunto-osakeyhtio are registered in Finland's electronic share register (osakehuoneistorekisteri) maintained by the National Land Survey (Maanmittauslaitos), which replaced the old paper share certificates in 2019.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Buying an As Oy apartment without reading the isannoitsijantodistus is the single biggest mistake Finnish buyers make. Upcoming pipe renovations (putkiremontti) alone can cost EUR 30,000–80,000 per apartment.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Check the isannoitsijantodistus for the company's 'PTS' (pitkan tahtaimen suunnitelma — long-term maintenance plan). This 10-year forward plan shows what major renovations are planned and when. It's the company's equivalent of a condition survey.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a putkiremontti?

A pipe renovation (plumbing overhaul) — one of the most expensive and disruptive renovations a Finnish housing company can undertake. It typically costs EUR 500–1,000 per square metre and forces residents out for several months. Companies typically face one every 40–50 years.

What is the taloyhtion velka?

The company debt (housing company loan) taken out to finance renovations. You pay your share through a 'rahoitusvastike' (financial charge) in addition to the hoitovastike. Both must be factored into monthly costs.

Can foreigners own As Oy shares?

Yes. There are no restrictions on EU or non-EU citizens purchasing shares in Finnish housing companies for residential use.
