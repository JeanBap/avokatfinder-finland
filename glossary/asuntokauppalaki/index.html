Asuntokauppalaki

Asuntokauppalaki Finland – Housing Trade Act and Buyer Protections

The Asuntokauppalaki (Housing Trade Act, 1994) is the primary legislation governing the sale of housing shares (asunto-osakkeet) and new-build housing in Finland. It establishes mandatory disclosure obligations for sellers, buyer remedies for defects, and special protections for purchasers of newly built housing from developers.

For sales of asunto-osakkeet (housing company shares), sellers must disclose all material information about the flat and the asunto-osakeyhtiö (housing company). The estate agent or seller must provide the buyer with a kuntotarkastusraportti (condition survey) or equivalent disclosure before the sale. Concealment of known defects gives the buyer the right to price reduction or contract cancellation.

For new-build housing from developers, the Asuntokauppalaki provides particularly strong protection. Developers must maintain a building warranty (rakennusvirhetakuu) for ten years from completion, covering major structural defects. They must also provide security (vakuus) through a bank guarantee or insurance during the construction period, protecting buyers if the developer becomes insolvent.

The Act distinguishes between defects in condition (kunto) and defects in legal title (oikeudellinen virhe). Condition defects entitle the buyer to price reduction, repair costs, or contract cancellation. Legal title defects — such as encumbrances not disclosed by the seller — give rise to stronger remedies including full rescission.

Claims for defects under the Asuntokauppalaki must be notified to the seller within a reasonable time of discovery (reklamaatiovelvollisuus). For asunto-osakkeet, the general limitation period for hidden defects is two years from handover; for new builds, ten years for major structural defects. Failure to give timely notice bars the claim.

⚠ Common MistakeFailing to notify the seller of a defect promptly after discovery. Delayed reklamaatio extinguishes the right to claim — in Finland, courts interpret the reklamaatiovelvollisuus strictly.
💡 Expert TipCommission a kuntotarkastus (condition survey) by an independent inspector before agreeing the final price. The survey cost is modest relative to potential defect risks, and its findings provide a basis for price negotiation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the reklamaatiovelvollisuus?

The legal obligation to notify the seller of a discovered defect within a reasonable time. In practice, Finnish courts have found delays of a few months to be acceptable in some cases, but prompt notification is always advisable.

Does the Asuntokauppalaki apply to rural real estate (kiinteistöt)?

No. Asuntokauppalaki applies to housing company shares and new builds. The sale of rural real estate (farmland, houses on privately owned land) is governed by the Maakaari (Real Estate Code).

Can a seller exclude liability under Asuntokauppalaki?

Seller liability for defects can be modified but not fully excluded in consumer sales. For commercial transactions between businesses, more extensive exclusions are permitted.
