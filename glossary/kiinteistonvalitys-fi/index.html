Kiinteistonvalitys

Kiinteistonvalitys is the Finnish term for real estate brokerage — the professional service provided by a licensed estate agent (kiinteistonvalittaja) in facilitating the sale or purchase of property.

In Finland, real estate agents (kiinteistonvalittajat) must hold a qualification certificate (LKV — Laillistettu kiinteistonvalittaja) and be registered with the Central Chamber of Commerce's Real Estate Agents Board (Kiinteistonvalittajien auktoriointilautakunta). The profession is regulated to protect consumers from unqualified intermediaries.

A Finnish estate agent typically acts for the seller and is paid a commission (provisio) of 2–4% of the sale price, inclusive of VAT. The commission is earned upon conclusion of a binding preliminary agreement (kauppakirja) or final deed. Some agents offer fixed-fee or hybrid models.

The agent is legally obliged to provide the buyer with all material information about the property, including any known defects, legal restrictions, and the results of condition surveys. Failure to disclose known material facts creates liability for the agent and may give the buyer grounds to rescind the transaction.

Buyers should request the kotilomake (property information sheet) and any kuntotarkastusraportti (condition survey report) from the agent. These documents summarise the property's characteristics and known condition issues. Agents must provide accurate information; misleading marketing creates civil liability.

The Finnish Consumer Disputes Board (Kuluttajariitalautakunta) handles disputes between consumers and real estate agents. More serious complaints go to the Real Estate Agents Board. Both provide relatively accessible and inexpensive dispute resolution compared to court proceedings.

⚠ Common MistakeSome sellers use unlicensed private intermediaries to save on commission. Without an LKV-licensed agent, consumer protection obligations do not apply in the same way, and dispute resolution options are more limited. The small saving on commission is rarely worth the risk.
💡 Expert TipAlways verify the agent's LKV certificate number before instructing them. The Central Chamber of Commerce publishes a public register of licensed agents. An LKV-certified agent is bound by professional conduct rules and carries professional liability insurance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I sell my Finnish property without an agent?

Yes. Private sales (yksityinen kauppa) are legal and relatively common in Finland, particularly in rural areas. The parties must still use a public purchase deed (julkinen kaupanvahvistaja) for the official deed.

Who pays the estate agent's commission?

By convention the seller pays the agent's commission. Buyers do not typically pay a fee unless they specifically engage a buyer's agent service.

What if the agent provided false information that affected my decision to buy?

You may have a claim against the agent for damages under the Act on Real Estate Agents (kiinteistonvalitysliikkeista annettu laki). Report to the Consumer Disputes Board within the relevant limitation period.
