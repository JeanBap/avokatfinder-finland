Hallinnanjakosopimus

A hallinnanjakosopimus is a Finnish co-ownership management agreement that divides the exclusive right to use specific parts of a jointly owned property among co-owners, without splitting the formal title.

In Finland, when two or more persons own a property jointly (yhteisomistus), they may enter a hallinnanjakosopimus (management division agreement) that allocates exclusive possession of specific areas — such as individual apartments, floors, or outbuildings — to each co-owner without changing the undivided ownership recorded in the land register.

The agreement is highly practical for properties that would be difficult to formally partition: a house with two self-contained dwellings, a farm with multiple buildings, or a plot divided between family members. Each co-owner gains certainty about which area they may use exclusively, while formal co-ownership continues.

For a hallinnanjakosopimus to be binding on future owners and creditors, it must be registered in the Kiinteistotietojarjestelma (Real Property Information System, formerly the land register). An unregistered agreement is binding between the parties but may not bind a buyer who purchases one co-owner's share without knowledge of the arrangement.

Banks lending against a co-owner's share must be made aware of the hallinnanjakosopimus, as it affects the security value of the share. The lender needs to understand which specific part of the property is associated with the co-owner's share, since a mortgage over an undivided share without an allocation agreement is very difficult to realise.

Buyers purchasing one share of a co-owned property should always check whether a hallinnanjakosopimus exists and is registered. The agreement defines what the buyer will actually occupy and use. Purchasing a share without understanding the management allocation can lead to disputes with co-owners over access and use.

⚠ Common MistakeBuyers of co-owned shares sometimes assume their share translates automatically to a specific apartment or area. Without a registered hallinnanjakosopimus, all co-owners have equal rights to the whole property. Always verify whether an allocation agreement exists and is registered.
💡 Expert TipIf buying into a co-owned property with the intention of occupying a specific area, insist that the hallinnanjakosopimus is registered in the land register before completion. An unregistered agreement is invisible to future buyers, creditors, and enforcement authorities.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a hallinnanjakosopimus the same as a partition (ositus)?

No. Partition formally divides the property into separate parcels with individual titles. A hallinnanjakosopimus keeps formal co-ownership intact but allocates exclusive use rights.

Can a hallinnanjakosopimus be changed?

Yes, by agreement of all co-owners. Changes should be registered to be effective against third parties.

What happens if a co-owner violates the agreement and uses another co-owner's area?

The affected co-owner can seek a court injunction and damages. A registered agreement provides stronger legal tools for enforcement.
