Kiinnitysrekisteri

Definition: Finnish property register that records ownership and encumbrances on real estate.

Overview

Kiinnitysrekisteri is Finland's official property registry maintained by the Land Registry (Maarekisterikeskus), recording ownership and all legal interests in real property. It provides transparent, publicly accessible evidence of property ownership and encumbrances.

Each Finnish property receives a unique land register identification (kiinteistötunnus) that remains constant throughout ownership changes. This system ensures clarity and prevents disputes over property identity.

The kiinnitysrekisteri records the current owner, any mortgages (kiinnitykset), liens, easements, and restrictions affecting the property. This comprehensive system protects both owners and creditors by clearly showing all encumbrances.

When property changes hands in Finland, the transfer must be registered in the kiinnitysrekisteri. This registration is essential to establish legal ownership and ensure the new owner's rights are recognized.

The kiinnitysrekisteri is open to public inspection, allowing prospective buyers, creditors, and interested parties to verify property ownership and identify any mortgages or other claims before engaging in transactions.

Key Facts

  • Maintained by Finnish Land Registry (Maarekisterikeskus)
  • Each property has unique kiinteistötunnus
  • Records ownership, mortgages, liens, and easements
  • Provides public property information access
  • Updated when ownership or encumbrances change

Common Mistake

Watch out: Not reviewing the kiinnitysrekisteri before purchasing Finnish property, potentially buying land with hidden mortgages.

Pro Tip

💡 Always request an official extract from the Land Registry before purchasing to verify clear title.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I access the Finnish kiinnitysrekisteri?

Visit Maarekisterikeskus online or in person with the property kiinteistötunnus to request an official extract.

What information is recorded in the kiinnitysrekisteri?

Current owner, mortgages, liens, easements, and other registered restrictions or legal interests.

How long does registration take in Finland?

Typically 2-4 weeks after proper documents are filed with the Land Registry.
