Kiinnitys

Definition: Finnish mortgage or security interest registered against real property.

Overview

Kiinnitys is the Finnish term for a mortgage or security interest in real property. When a property owner borrows money, the lender typically requires a kiinnitys to secure the loan with the property as collateral.

A kiinnitys is registered in the kiinnitysrekisteri (property registry) and creates a legal claim against the property. Upon borrower default, the lender can initiate foreclosure proceedings to seize and sell the property.

Multiple kiinnitykset can be registered against the same property, each with a priority ranking by registration date. First-registered mortgages have priority in receiving proceeds from property sales.

Finnish law strictly regulates the amount of kiinnitys a lender can require, typically limiting it to the property's assessed value. Lenders must follow strict disclosure and documentation requirements.

Once a kiinnitys is satisfied, the lender must formally discharge it from the property registry. Until discharged, the lender retains a legal claim, even if the debt has been satisfied.

Key Facts

  • Mortgage or security interest on Finnish property
  • Registered in kiinnitysrekisteri
  • Creates priority claim if property foreclosed
  • Amount limited to property value
  • Must be discharged when debt is satisfied

Common Mistake

Watch out: Not fully understanding the terms of kiinnitys, including interest rates, payment schedules, and foreclosure rights.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can multiple kiinnitykset be placed on one property?

Yes. Multiple mortgages may be registered, with priority determined by registration order.

What recourse does a lender have if I default on kiinnitys?

The lender can initiate foreclosure (ulosottomenettely) to sell the property and recover the debt.

How do I remove a kiinnitys after paying my loan?

Request the lender formally discharge (poista) the mortgage from the kiinnitysrekisteri.
